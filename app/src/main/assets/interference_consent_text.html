You are being asked to volunteer in a research study. Your participation is solely voluntary and opting out of the measurement will not affect your ability to use other features on the app, e.g. bandwidth tests.

This project will document and analyze network interference practices around the world, including Internet interference and censorship. You may view aggregated reports of these filtering practices from www.internetcensorshiplab.org, though there is no direct benefit to you other than the public good of providing transparency about network interference.

The measurements will access a list of web pages and services and record data from these tests. These tests will not provide any information about you to the researchers and should not affect your browsing experience. However, these measurements will appear to come from you and you may incur the following risks if you join the study: Your ISP or network administrator may be able to see the list of websites being accessed by the measurement software.

  • There is a risk that accessing such websites and running these tests violates network policies.
  • There is a risk that accessing these sensitive websites is in violation of local laws or regulations.

The potential risks posed by conducting these tests are highly dependent on the domestic political and legal context in your country. While we know of no explicit laws that forbid the collection of network measurements on Internet censorship, the nature of this research can potentially have political impacts and could be seen unfavorably by certain authorities. If an authority views exposure as potentially damaging then nationals of that country who are found to be engaged in this study may be scrutinized by the authorities.


While it is challenging to definitively identify what the risks of conducting these tests may be in your country, you may click the link below to see an external measure of press and Internet freedom in your country:

Protecting Your Privacy in this Study

We will take steps to help make sure that all the information we get about you is kept private. We will never learn your name, though we will use a tag (unique alphanumerical user identifier) instead. All the study data that we get from you will be on a machine in a secured computer network that is only accessible to members of the research team. If any papers and talks are given about this research, neither your name nor your IP address will be used.

Experiment data may include issuing requests for URLs found in the URL list (similar to what a user does manually with a browser, such as Firefox), DNS queries (mapping hostnames such as Google.com to IP address such as 72.14.52.3), or traceroutes (measuring network paths). Experiments will only log network traffic associated with the experiment (e.g., network packets and timestamps).

Once an experiment has completed the resulting data will be sent, along with the experiment ID and user ID, to the data management system. Once the data management system confirms receipt of the data it will be removed from the client software. Sensitive data that will be collected and transmitted is the IP address of the user. Experiment results will be encrypted in transit to ensure that no identifiable information is leaked in transit.

Your Rights as a Research Subject

  • Your participation in this study is voluntary. You do not have to be in this study if you don't want to be.
  • You have the right to change your mind and leave the study at any time without giving any reason, and without penalty.
  • Any new information that may make you change your mind about being in this study will be given to you.
